Will + infinitive and going to + infinitive are both used to talk about the future.
Going to is used when there is a link to the present, for example if something is already planned or a change has started:
Mall owners are going to need to think of new ideas to remain in business.
We see from the text that many malls have already started thinking of new ideas.
Will + infinitive is used for prediction: something the writer believes about the future. It does not
have to be based on present evidence:
the mall will go from strength to strength.
This is the writer's prediction or belief about the future.
